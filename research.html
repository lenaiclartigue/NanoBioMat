Research overview

Our research lies at the interface of chemistry, physics and biology. We design magnetic iron oxide nanoparticle-based nanoassemblies for biomedical applications, with a particular focus on magnetic hyperthermia, controlled drug delivery and nanomedicine strategies for liver diseases.

Magnetic nanoassemblies

We investigate how the spatial organization of iron oxide nanoparticles within dense assemblies influences their magnetic interactions and their heating efficiency under alternating magnetic fields.

Magnetically activated nanomedicine

We develop magnetically responsive nanoassemblies combining magnetic nanoparticles and therapeutic molecules, with the aim of triggering or enhancing therapeutic effects through magnetic stimulation.

Nanomedicine for liver diseases

We explore the potential of magnetic nanoassemblies for the treatment of liver diseases, including hepatic cancer and immune-related hepatic disorders.
