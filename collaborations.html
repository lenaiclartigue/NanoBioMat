Collaborations

NanoBioMat is built through national and international collaborations combining expertise in magnetic nanoparticles, polymer chemistry, magnetic characterization, nanomedicine, cell biology and liver disease models.

National collaborations

Magnetic nanomedicine and biophysics

Collaborations focused on magnetic hyperthermia, magnetic properties of nanoparticles in biological environments, imaging and biophysical characterization.

Liver diseases and immunology

Collaborations dedicated to hepatic cancer, immune-mediated liver diseases, cellular models, 3D liver models and preclinical evaluation of nanoassemblies.

Photoactive nanoassemblies

Collaborations aimed at combining magnetic nanoparticles with photosensitizers or photoactive molecules for multimodal therapeutic strategies.

International collaborations

Polymer-stabilized magnetic nanocarriers

International collaborations focus on the design of magnetic nanocarriers stabilized by polymers, including polysaccharide-based and functional polymer shells.

Magnetic interactions and structural characterization

Collaborative projects aim to understand the structure and magnetic coupling within dense multicore nanoassemblies using complementary techniques such as magnetic measurements, scattering methods and advanced nanoscale characterization.

Collaborative expertise

  • Iron oxide nanoparticle synthesis and surface functionalization
  • Polymer chemistry and colloidal stabilization
  • Magnetic hyperthermia and magnetothermal measurements
  • Magnetic characterization and nanoscale structure analysis
  • Cellular studies and nanoparticle-biointerface analysis
  • 3D biological models and liver-oriented nanomedicine
  • Preclinical evaluation of therapeutic nanoassemblies
